Image File Formats

Click on any image to view it in full screen.

JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group)
Best for photographs and complex images with many colors. Uses lossy compression.
PNG (Portable Network Graphics)
Supports transparency and lossless compression. Good for logos and graphics with sharp edges.
GIF (Graphics Interchange Format)
Limited to 256 colors. Supports simple animation and transparency. Good for simple icons.
GIF with Text Overlay
Demonstrates text over an image. GIF is often used for banners and simple animations.
